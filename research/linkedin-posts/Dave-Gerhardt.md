## Post 1 (most recent in this snapshot) — Great marketers take inspiration everywhere (“no formula”)

**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_great-marketers-know-how-to-take-inspiration-activity-7418740024793501696-fOAo  

**Title (AI summary on public page):** Marketing in Unique Contexts: Crafting Success Without a Formula  

**Context shown on public page:** Exit Five • ~198K followers • “2mo” relative to page snapshot  

**Body:**

Great marketers know how to take inspiration from everywhere. They are curious. Creative. Scrappy. They don't need "B2B marketing examples" to understand what might work in a B2B context. I think one of the best parts of the job is marketing is that the ingredients are almost always different. Open the fridge and you've got to make a meal with three eggs, half a bagel, and an onion. But then at another company, the fridge is stocked full with fresh produce, grass fed beef, and a freshly tapped keg of kombucha (or something like that). At each company the ingredients are different. Some companies are well funded. Some have no funding. Some companies have proven CEOs. Some have first-time CEOs. Sometimes you can attract Tier 1 talent (ex: Anthropic right now) and sometimes you can't because of the things i wrote before this; you might have to figure out how to start with Tier 3 talent and make it work. Some companies are in super regulated industries. Some have fewer rules. But this is what's FUN. There is no formula. It's always different. Channels that worked for one company don't work for another. This is the craft of marketing. "Put me anywhere on God's Green Earth, I could triple my worth." - Jay-Z That is how great marketers think too. Any company, any stage, any industry - they could figure it out.
