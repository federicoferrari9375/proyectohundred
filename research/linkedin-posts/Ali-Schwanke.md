## Post 2 — Managing AI agents and teams

**Title (AI summary on public page):** Managing AI agents and teams: A new challenge for leaders  

**Context shown on public page:** Knightscope • ~19K followers • “6mo” relative to page snapshot  

**Body:**

I know how to manage people. But how do you manage AI agents? Or teams of agents talking to agents? Business leadership and executives are in for a wild ride. And it's only beginning. For many years we've developed teams of people, systems to make decisions, guardrails, coaching/performance evals, etc. And that still part of our job as leaders - to delegate, manage and direct outcomes. But we're now managing AI, AI agents, and AI agent teams working with other AI agent teams. And soon they'll be conversing and making decisions without us but we're still held responsible for the outcomes of those AI to AI decisions and initiatives. Many of us at the moment are under the false assumption now that we can just "let AI do the work" and "AI can do what 5 humans used to" (whether we like to admit it or not) - but AI needs to be managed, and the very same type of delegation and monitoring of decisions, frameworks and downstream effects will take a whole new shape. For example - with humans, many high performing teams have development plans, paired with coaching, certifications, and bonuses. We have performance scorecards and job expectations. How will we do this with AI? Does AI need to be "coached? How will we manage the decisions it makes with other agents and quantify the impact of that decisions? How do we teach it new things and prove that it knows what it's doing? How do we reprimand? Does AI get a PIP? (funny but not really) We'd never let a human sit idle in an office after their initial 90 day onboarding - but I'm seeing this creeping into our world now as we've never managed autonomous learning bots. If we're trying to use the same leadership and management practices that got us here, I'm afraid we're in for a bumpy ride. That being said, none of us are quite certain what the future holds but can only identify the new problems being faced and how we solve those and manage those resources to drive outcomes. Curious to hear people's take on how leaders manage AI agents and teams of AI agents - not so much philosophy, but even if there's a practical exercise you're doing now to set the foundation. ____ P.S. this comes fresh from an amazing panel discussion with MarTech this morning, along with Jessica Kao, Mike Pastore, AJ Sedlak, Julz James, Verl Allen. My wheels are turning!
